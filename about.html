Don't repeat yourself (DRY)

Don't Repeat Yourself (DRY) is a fundamental principle in programming that emphasizes reducing redundancy in code. The idea is simple: avoid duplicating logic or functionality by abstracting repetitive tasks into reusable components, functions, or modules. By following DRY, code becomes more maintainable, efficient, and easier to update. Changes need to be made in just one place, reducing the risk of errors and improving overall clarity. This principle encourages writing clean, modular, and scalable code, making it a cornerstone of good software development practices.
